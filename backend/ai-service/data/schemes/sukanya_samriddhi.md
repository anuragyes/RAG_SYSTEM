# Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

## Overview
Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is a small savings scheme under the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao campaign. It encourages parents to build a fund for the future education and marriage expenses of their girl child. It offers one of the highest interest rates among government savings schemes.

## Eligibility Criteria
- **Account holder**: Girl child below 10 years of age
- **Guardian**: Natural or legal guardian opens the account on behalf of the girl child
- **Maximum accounts**: Only 2 accounts per family (one for each girl child)
  - Exception: 3rd account allowed in case of twins/triplets for the second birth
- **Citizenship**: Must be an Indian citizen and resident of India
- **NRI/PIO**: Not eligible

## Benefits
- **Interest Rate**: 8.2% per annum (Q1 FY 2024-25), compounded yearly — among highest government rates
- **Tax Benefits**: Triple tax exempt (EEE status under Section 80C):
  - Deposit amount eligible for deduction up to ₹1.5 Lakh/year
  - Interest earned is tax-free
  - Maturity amount is tax-free
- **Minimum Deposit**: ₹250 per year
- **Maximum Deposit**: ₹1.5 Lakh per year
- **Maturity Period**: 21 years from date of opening OR when girl gets married after age 18
- **Partial Withdrawal**: 50% of balance allowed after girl turns 18 for higher education
- **Premature Closure**: Allowed after girl turns 18 for marriage

## How to Apply
1. Visit any Post Office or authorized commercial bank (SBI, PNB, BOB, ICICI, Axis, etc.)
2. Fill SSY Account Opening Form
3. Submit required documents
4. Make initial deposit of minimum ₹250

## Required Documents
- Birth certificate of the girl child
- Identity proof of parent/guardian (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport)
- Address proof of parent/guardian
- Passport-size photographs (parent and child)

## Important Rules
- Deposits must be made for minimum 15 years from account opening
- If minimum ₹250 not deposited in a year, penalty of ₹50
- Account can be transferred anywhere in India
